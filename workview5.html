INSIDE-OUT ART MUSEUM
Typography, Branding
2011

The positioning of the Inside-out Art Museum mainly aims to create a protection zone for traditional art as well as a paradise for the burgeoning of modern thoughts, paying special attention and reflection to both the West and the East. Since it is an art gallery without the clear boundary, it greatly coincides with the definition of the dotted line. The dotted line, neither traditional nor modern, neither the East nor the West, owns its function in the daily life. While being detached with the simple aesthetic definition, it possesses wider and more energetic extensibility. I applied the abstract symbol-dotted line in the design, which supplements the concept of the Inside-out Art Museum with a natural symbiotic relationship. The concept of the dotted line is applied to form the visual image of the organization in VI design with powerful inclusiveness. Instead of being confined to a single figure, conceptual image uses the dotted line as the symbol that has certain and an extended meaning, which is regarded as the starting point for people to understand and think.
